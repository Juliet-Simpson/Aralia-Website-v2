How does it work?

The 3D Illuminator uses Photometric Stereo (PS) illumination and Thermography to capture and process images, either locally on the phone or via the cloud, transmitted off site for a remote assessment by expert review. Also included is the facility to archive, display and automatically assess images using AI.

It works ‘out of the box’ with minimal training and all the necessary software easy to download. Its simplicity and low price point enables high volumes to be readily distributed whenever necessary.

Solves an industry problem

To keep large assets operational, companies rely on Condition Monitoring (CM) and Non-Destructive testing (NDT) regimes to determine the need for maintenance or repairs. This extends asset lifespan, reducing the need for capital investment to replace integral equipment and prevents operational failures. Over 25,000 inspections are carried out every day in the UK.

Aralia's 3D Illuminator can enable CM and NDT professionals to quickly and accurately identify surface defects as a first step in the testing process. It is easy to use on site for initial assessments to determine if additional resources or more rigorous testing is required, making it a useful aid. Unlike liquid penetrant testing, where the dye has to sit and fully absorb into the surface (taking between one and three hours), Aralia's solution works within minutes.

3D information enhances the accuracy of AI solutions for condition monitoring. Using a simple AI tool the 3D Illuminator can perform any initial analysis locally and send images remotely off site for expert review.
